The Plant That Eats Bugs

Most plants get nutrients from soil. But the Venus Flytrap is different. It is a meat-eater!

Why Eat Bugs?

Nutrient Acquisition Strategy

Plants usually get their food from the soil. However, Venus Flytraps live in poor soil that lacks essential nutrients like Nitrogen. To survive, they have adapted to become carnivorous, getting their vitamins from insects instead of the dirt.

Fig 1. Nutrient Sources: Standard Plants vs. Flytraps

What's on the Menu?

Typical Diet Composition

While they are called "Flytraps," they actually eat more crawling bugs than flying ones! Ants and spiders are their favorite meals because they crawl right into the trap.
