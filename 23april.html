📚 April 23rd · World Book Day

William Shakespeare

✨ A day to honour books, authors, and the magic of reading.

On this date in 1616, two of the world’s greatest writers — William Shakespeare and Miguel de Cervantes — passed away. Their stories live on.

📖 UNESCO celebrates this day as World Book and Copyright Day. Let’s explore!

Miguel de Cervantes

“Though she be but little, she is fierce.” — Shakespeare

🌍 Why April 23rd?

UNESCO World Book and Copyright Day

  • Both Shakespeare and Cervantes died on April 23, 1616 (though calendars differed slightly).
  • Also St. George’s Day in England — a day of legends and dragons!
  • A worldwide celebration of reading, publishing, and copyright.
  • In Catalonia, it’s traditional to give a book and a rose.

✨ For children: books open doors to courage, kindness, and imagination.
