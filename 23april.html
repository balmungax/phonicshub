A day to honour books, authors, and the magic of storytelling. On this date in 1616, two of history's greatest writers — Shakespeare and Cervantes — both passed away, leaving a legacy that still lives in every library on Earth.

The date · UNESCO

Why April 23rd?

✍️

Two Giants, One Day

Shakespeare and Cervantes both died on April 23, 1616 — though on different calendar systems, the symbolism was irresistible.

🌹

Sant Jordi Tradition

In Catalonia it's St. George's Day — people traditionally exchange a book and a rose, blending legend with literature.

🏛️

UNESCO Since 1995

The UN declared it World Book and Copyright Day, celebrating reading, publishing, and the protection of intellectual heritage.

📚

For Young Readers

Books open doors to courage, curiosity, and empathy — in every language, every culture, every classroom on Earth.

Meet the Authors

William Shakespeare

1564 – 1616  ·  England

  • 37 plays & 154 sonnets
  • Globe Theatre, London
  • Invented 1,700+ English words
  • Hamlet, Romeo & Juliet

Miguel de Cervantes

1547 – 1616  ·  Spain

  • Father of the modern novel
  • Don Quixote (1605 & 1615)
  • Fought at the Battle of Lepanto
  • Spent 5 years captive in Algiers
