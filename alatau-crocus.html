The Brave Flower That Grows Through Snow!
The Alatau Crocus Flower

Meet the Brave Alatau Crocus!

This is one of the bravest little plants in the world! It doesn't wait for summer. It wakes up while there is still ice and snow on the ground!

Its full name is Crocus alatavicus. People call it the "Ice Breaker" flower because it's so tough!

Cool Fact

In the Alatau mountains, people love these flowers so much that they are protected. You can look, but don't pick them!

Growing through snow!

The Mountain Explorer

This crocus lives high up in the mountains of Central Asia. It grows in places like Kazakhstan and China.

Its home is called the Alatau Mountains. That's how it got its name!

While other plants are still sleeping under a cold white blanket of snow, the Alatau Crocus is already stretching its petals.

Snow Power

It grows so fast that it often pokes its head right through the melting snow. It's like an alarm clock for spring!

Look inside the flower!

A Fancy "Secret" Outfit

If you saw this flower from far away, you might think it's just a plain white cup. But look closer!

Inside: The petals are bright, snowy white with a glowing yellow center. It looks like a little sunny-side-up egg!

Outside: The back of the petals are covered in grey and purple spots. It's like the flower is wearing a polka-dot raincoat to stay warm.

Color Secret

The yellow center helps attract bees who come to visit the flower!

A special mountain treasure

Why is it so Special?

Snow-Melter: It grows right through melting snow. It tells all the animals that spring is coming!

Bee Breakfast: It's the first flower to bloom. It gives hungry bees their first meal after winter.

The "Corm" Power-Up: Under the dirt, the plant has a little round ball called a corm. This is like a tiny battery pack filled with food energy. It helps the flower grow through frozen ground!

Power Source

The corm stores food from last year. This gives the flower energy to grow when it's still cold outside!
